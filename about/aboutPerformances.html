History

CMU Raasta began with the vision of three people to bring a south asian cultural phenomenon to Carnegie Mellon University. Since 2011, CMU Raasta has been committed to promoting Indian culture across campus by performing on and off campus at dance competitions throughout the nation. By exhibiting the talent and diversity of Carnegie Mellon students, we aim to spread the awareness of Gujurati traditions through the West Indian folk dance Garba-Raas.

Please come support us at our local and national events every year! We perform not only on the campus but also at national-level competitions along with private parties such as weddings, receptions, etc.


Dance Style

The Garba-Raas dance style has formed through the joining of two distinct styles: Garba [link: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garba_(dance)] (based on circular dancing) and Raas [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dandiya_Raas] (where sticks, dandiya, are twirled). Raas and Garba are typical Gujurati folk dances that are energetic and involve whirling and moving their feet and arms in a complicated, choreographed manner.

Garba-raas music is typically very dynamic, involving various voices and percussion instruments to create various rhythms. At the competitive level, Garba-Raas teams will create mixes that are blends of western and traditional Gujurati music to match their spirited choreography, formations, and props.

While dancing Garba-Raas, women wear chaniya cholis, colorful skirts and blouses often with hand embroidery and embedded mirrors and shells. Women also often wear traditional Gujurati silver jewelry.
